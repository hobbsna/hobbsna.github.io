The Handbell Guru

Established 1923

History:

The first handbells were developed by Robert and William Cor. They were the first to tune bells, giving them an accurate fundamental tone. Originally, handbells were used by tower bell ringers for practice purposes. In 1902, Margaret Shurcliff (of England) brings handbells to the United States. Today, handbells are mostly seen in churches, but there are non-religious groups that use them as well.

Playing Technique:

Handbells are played in a circular motion, first away and then back towards you. You "dampen" the bells by resting them just above your shoulder blade. One side of the handle has a small bell symbol. This should always point towards you, as it will make the bell sound more clear. There are more advanced techniques and articulations, but we won't cover that here.

Maintenance and Care:

Always wear gloves when handling handbells. The oils on your hand can tarnish the brass. Bells should be stored somewhere where they won't rub against each other, as that the chips away the brass. Most handbell companies provide specially made cases for them. Occasionally, the clapper part of the bell breaks or a screw comes loose. This is nothing to worry about, although it is a good idea to get them repaired by a professional, if possible. Lastly, use a polishing cloth if you want to keep your bells looking new.

Recommended Accessories:

  • Music Stands. To hold your music for you.
  • Stand lights. For dark performances.
  • Mallets. Good for songs that use the staccato articulation.
  • Handbell tree. Holds many handbells and is particularly useful if you have heavy mallet sections.
